# Role-Specific 30-60-90 Day Plans

Reference file for the hr-onboarding skill. Load this file when generating plans
for roles outside of engineering (design, sales, marketing, ops, finance, etc.).

---

## Sales / Business Development

### Days 1–30
- Complete product training and demo certification
- Shadow 5+ customer calls (discovery, demo, closing)
- Learn CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot) — log your first 10 contacts
- Understand ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) and current pipeline health

### Days 31–60
- Own a small set of accounts / leads from the pipeline
- Run your first solo discovery call
- Contribute to one outbound campaign
- Understand the full sales motion from lead to close

### Days 61–90
- Close your first deal (even a small one)
- Propose one improvement to current sales messaging or sequence
- Participate in a quarterly business review (QBR)

---

## Marketing / Growth

### Days 1–30
- Review last 12 months of campaigns — what worked, what didn't
- Audit all active channels (SEO, paid, social, email)
- Meet with product and sales for alignment
- Get access to all analytics tools and set up dashboards

### Days 31–60
- Own one campaign end-to-end (ideation → launch → report)
- Propose a hypothesis and run a small A/B test
- Publish at least one piece of content (blog, email, LinkedIn)

### Days 61–90
- Present performance insights to leadership
- Propose Q+1 marketing experiments
- Identify one new channel or tactic worth testing

---

## Finance / Accounts

### Days 1–30
- Understand chart of accounts, close cycle, and reporting cadence
- Shadow month-end close and reconciliation process
- Meet with all key stakeholders who submit expenses or budgets

### Days 31–60
- Independently run one reporting cycle (daily/weekly report)
- Audit one process for efficiency improvements
- Understand P&L structure and how to explain variances

### Days 61–90
- Propose one automation or process improvement
- Own a budget reforecast for one department
- Present one financial insight to leadership

---

## People Ops / HR

### Days 1–30
- Learn the HRIS system thoroughly
- Review all active policies, handbooks, and compliance docs
- Shadow one hiring process end-to-end
- Meet all team leads / department heads

### Days 31–60
- Own one HR process (e.g., onboarding, exit interviews)
- Identify one policy gap or improvement
- Run your first employee engagement touchpoint

### Days 61–90
- Implement one HR initiative (even small scale)
- Complete a culture/engagement pulse survey
- Present findings from first 90 days to CHRO/CEO

---

## Operations / Program Management

### Days 1–30
- Map all ongoing projects and their status
- Understand cross-functional dependencies
- Identify top 3 operational bottlenecks
- Learn internal project tracking tools

### Days 31–60
- Own one operational workstream independently
- Run one cross-functional sync meeting
- Document one process that currently exists only in people's heads

### Days 61–90
- Ship one operational improvement that saves time or reduces errors
- Present an ops dashboard or health report to leadership
- Identify the next highest-priority project to own
